Type of product#

A product is defined by a model type (e.g. cosmo), a model name (e.g. cosmo-leps), and a product category (e.g. eps member). Product characteristics are derived from the meta-information available in the input records; interpretation of this information is dependent on local conventions defined by each originating center. Products from the following center are correctly and exhaustively interpreted:

  • Zurich, Offenbach (all local coding)

  • Roma (some local coding, COSMO-LEPS)

  • ARPA-SIMC, Moscow (generic)

  • COSMO (generic, COSMO-LEPS)

  • ECMWF (generic, some local coding)

When the product characteristics are not recognized, the product characteristics can be explicitly set by the user. In particular, a generic model name ‘cosmo’ is provided to support any center using the cosmo model. When working with GRIB 1 input, some local coding may still not be accessible, in particular EPS related information. To overcome this limitation, it is supposed that the coding of EPS information in GRIB 1, associated with the generic model name ‘cosmo’, follows the COSMO-LEPS standard.

A product is fully supported when the product is recognized, the coding of vertical coordinates is recognized, the procedures to reconstruct the vertical coordinates are implemented (when relevant), and the EPS informations are recognized (when relevant).

In this document, one refers to EPS derived products as PDF related fields (PDF stands for Probability Distribution Function).

It is possible to work with unsupported or unrecognized products by setting ‘strict_usage’ to false in &RunSpecification. Most fieldextra operations will still be allowed; however, some information may not be decoded (local usage), which may lead to program exceptions, and some operations may lead to incorrect results, so double check your output!
